budget, the marginalist auction incentivized efficiency. It forced corporations to compete by optimizing their plants: the one that
managed to produce slightly cheaper than the average increased its profit margin, while obsolete plants were squeezed out of the
market. In a world of analog scarcity and linear technologies, the system functioned as an acceptable cost regulator.
The problem is that the context changed completely, and what was born as a tool for optimization has been transformed into an

marginal-cost sources should cause electricity prices to collapse to ridiculous levels.

for a dollar, and having your entire bill charged at checkout based on the price of the very last potato sold, which
